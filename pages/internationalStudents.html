International Student Guide: Study in NZ

Welcome! New Zealand universities offer world-class education and a unique, welcoming culture. We've broken down the essential steps and requirements for students coming from overseas.

1. Visas and Admission Requirements

Securing your admission and student visa are the most critical steps before you travel.

  • The Student Visa

    You must apply for a Student Visa through Immigration New Zealand (INZ). You need an unconditional offer of place from your university, proof of funds, and insurance.

    Visit Immigration NZ
  • English Language Proficiency

    All universities require proof of English skills, typically an: IELTS Academic (usually 6.0–6.5 overall), TOEFL iBT, or PTE Academic. Check your chosen course requirements carefully.

  • International Qualifications

    Your high school results (e.g., A-Levels, IB, SATs) must be assessed to meet the NZ University Entrance standard. The university's International Admissions team will handle this assessment.

2. Funding Your Study and Living Costs

International students pay higher tuition fees and must demonstrate they can cover all living expenses.

Proof of Funds Requirement:

Immigration NZ requires you to show you have at least NZD $20,000 for each year of study (or $15,000 for courses less than a year) to cover living expenses, in addition to tuition fees.

  • Insurance: You must have approved health and travel insurance for the entire duration of your stay in New Zealand. This is a mandatory visa condition.
  • Working Rights: Most student visas allow you to work up to 20 hours per week during academic semesters and full-time during scheduled breaks. This is a great way to gain experience and help with living costs.
  • Tuition Fees: International fees are significantly higher than domestic fees. Always check the specific fee schedule on your university's website.

3. Adjusting to Life in New Zealand

New Zealand is safe and welcoming, but cultural differences exist. Here are tips for settling in smoothly:

  • Accommodation: Many international students choose Homestay (living with a local family) or a Catered Hall of Residence in their first year for easy adjustment.
  • University Support: Every NZ university has a dedicated International Student Support Office. Use their services for orientation, cultural advice, visa queries, and welfare support—they are your first point of contact!
  • Transport: Public transport is reliable in major cities (Auckland, Wellington, Christchurch). Invest in a student transport card for discounts.
