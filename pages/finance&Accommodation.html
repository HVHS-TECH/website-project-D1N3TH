Finance and Accommodation

Managing your finances and finding a place to live while studying

University life brings new financial responsibilities and housing choices. Here’s a breakdown of what you need to know:
  1. Student Loans and Allowances
    • StudyLink is the New Zealand government service for student financial support.
    • Apply for student loans to cover course fees and living costs.
    • Student allowances are available for eligible students based on income and circumstances.
  2. Accommodation Options
    • Halls of Residence: University-managed accommodation, often catered, great for first-year students.
    • Flatting: Renting a house or apartment with other students, offering more independence.
    • Homestay: Living with a local family, often chosen by international students for cultural immersion.
    • Private Rentals: Finding your own rental property.
  3. Budgeting and Living Costs
    • Create a budget to track your income and expenses.
    • Typical weekly costs include rent, food, utilities (power, internet), transport, and social activities.
    • Look for student discounts and part-time job opportunities to help manage costs.
