Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about university, student life, and more.

Here are answers to common university-related questions:

  1. What is the difference between undergraduate and postgraduate study?
    • Undergraduate = First degree (e.g. Bachelor’s)
    • Postgraduate = Advanced study after a Bachelor’s (e.g. Master’s, PhD)
  2. Do I need NCEA Level 3 for all universities?
    • Yes but some universities offer alternative pathways like foundation programs
  3. Can I change my university course after I start?
    • Yes, most universities allow you to change courses within the first few weeks of the semester. Some changes may require approval, and there might be deadlines, so check with your university’s student support services.
  4. Do I have to take out a student loan to study at university?
    • No, but most students use Student Loans to cover tuition fees. You can also apply for scholarships or work part-time to help with costs. If your family can support you financially, you may not need a loan.
