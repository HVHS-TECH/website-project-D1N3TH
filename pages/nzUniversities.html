The Eight New Zealand Universities

New Zealand is home to eight government-funded universities, all recognised globally for their high quality of education and research. Choosing where to study is a big decision, often based on location, programme strengths, and campus culture.

Location Overview: All eight universities are spread across the North and South Islands. Open Map View

Explore Your Options

Click the links below to go directly to the university's official website to start researching courses and entry requirements.

  • University of Auckland

    Location: Auckland (North Island)

    Known For: Largest university, strong in research, Medicine, Law, and Engineering.

  • Auckland University of Technology (AUT)

    Location: Auckland (North Island)

    Known For: Innovative, modern focus, strong ties to industry, Communications, Design, and Health Sciences.

  • University of Waikato

    Location: Hamilton & Tauranga (North Island)

    Known For: Education, Māori and Pacific Development, Computer Science, and Business Studies.

  • Massey University

    Location: Palmerston North, Wellington, & Auckland (Multiple Campuses)

    Known For: Specialises in Veterinary Science, Aviation, Agriculture, and New Zealand's largest provider of Distance Learning.

  • Victoria University of Wellington - Te Herenga Waka

    Location: Wellington (North Island)

    Known For: Located in the capital city, strong in Law, Political Science, Architecture, and Humanities.

  • University of Canterbury (UC)

    Location: Christchurch (South Island)

    Known For: Excellent Engineering (including the renowned College of Engineering), Forestry, Science, and strong on-campus student life.

  • Lincoln University

    Location: Christchurch (South Island)

    Known For: Highly specialised in the primary sector: Land-based studies, Agriculture, Agribusiness, and Horticulture.

  • University of Otago - Te Whare Wānanga o Otāgo

    Location: Dunedin (South Island)

    Known For: Oldest university in NZ, famous for Health Sciences (Medicine/Dentistry), Neuroscience, and a vibrant, traditional student culture.
