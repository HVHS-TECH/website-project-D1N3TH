Achieving University Entrance (UE) in New Zealand

Gaining University Entrance is the fundamental requirement for all domestic students seeking to enrol in a bachelor's degree at any New Zealand university. It proves you have the essential academic foundation to succeed in tertiary education.

The Core University Entrance Requirements (NCEA)

To offically qualify for University Entrance via NCEA, you must meet the following four criteria. These are the minimal national standards, and it is critical you meet all of them:

  • NCEA Level 3: Must have obtained the Level 3 NCEA qualification (60 credits at Level 3 + 20 credits at Level 2).
  • Approved Subjects: A minimum of 14 credits in three different subjects from the list of approved subjects.
  • Literacy: A minimum of 10 literacy credits (5 reading credits and 5 writing credits) gained from specified Level 2 or Level 3 standards.
  • Numeracy: A minimum of 10 numeracy credits gained from specified standards at Level 1 or higher.
Important: Meeting University Entrance (UE) requirements is the minimum standard. Many competitive courses (e.g. Law, Health Sciences, Engineering) and some universities require higher grades (e.g. specific Grade Point Averages or Ranking Scores).

Digging Deeper: Approved Subjects and Credit Details

The Approved Subjects are a specific list set by the New Zealand Qualifications Authority (NZQA). They are academic subjects like Maths, English, History, Sciences, etc., and generally do not include practical, vocational, or heavily unit standard-based courses.

Key Details on Approved Credits:

  • Why 14 Credits? You must achieve 14 credits at Level 3 in three different subjects from the approved list. This ensures depth of knowledge across a range of academic fields.
  • Where to find the list? The full list is published annually by NZQA. If you are unsure if your Level 3 subject is approved, check with your school's Dean or Careers Advisor, or the official NZQA website.
  • Excellence and Merit: While not required for UE, achieving Merit or Excellence endorsements significantly boosts your chances of gaining competitive entry to specific university programmes and securing scholarships.

Alternative Pathways to University Entrance

Not all students achieve UE through the NCEA pathway. Universities also accept qualifications like the Cambridge International Examinations (CIE) and the International Baccalaureate (IB).

  • CIE (Cambridge): Generally requires a minimum of 120 UCAS Tariff Points from three subjects at A or AS level, and specific grade passes in English language and Mathematics at IGCSE or AS level.
  • IB (International Baccalaureate): Requires the award of the IB Diploma, typically with a minimum score (often around 24-26 points, depending on the university).
  • Special Admission: If you are over 20 years old and do not meet the academic requirements, you may apply for "Special Admission" based on other experience.

Your Next Steps After Meeting UE

Achieving University Entrance is just the first checkpoint! You must still formally apply to the university of your choice for the specific degree programme you want to study.
